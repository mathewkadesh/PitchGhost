Partners ask a small set of questions in almost every first meeting. On the surface they sound like requests for information you've already put in the deck. Underneath, they're testing something else entirely.

## "How big can this really get?"

This is rarely a request for your TAM slide — they've seen it, and they know TAM slides are theatre. What they're actually testing is **whether you understand the mechanism by which this gets big**: is it a wedge into a much larger budget, a behaviour change that compounds, a platform that captures adjacent markets? Answer with the mechanism, not the market-size number.

## "Who else have you talked to?"

This question is doing two things at once: gauging competitive heat, and — more importantly — checking whether you're being straight with them. Naming firms that have passed, and *why* they passed, often lands better than implying universal interest. A founder who can explain a "no" with insight looks more investable than one who pretends every meeting went perfectly.

## "What would have to be true for this to fail?"

This is a test of self-awareness, not pessimism. Partners are wary of founders who can't articulate their own biggest risk — it suggests either naivety or a sales-mode that never switches off. A sharp answer names the real risk *and* what you're doing about it, even if the mitigation is partial. "We don't fully know yet, but here's how we'll find out in the next quarter" is often a better answer than false confidence.

## "Why now?"

This is the one founders most often answer with a macro trend slide, when the partner is usually asking something narrower: *why didn't this work three years ago, and why won't it be obvious to everyone in six months?* The strongest answers point to a specific unlock — a cost curve, a regulatory shift, a platform capability — that changes the equation in a way that's durable, not just timely.

## The pattern underneath all four

Every one of these questions is really asking: **does this founder understand their business as well as I'd need them to in order to bet on it without me in the room?** Answer that, and the specific question almost stops mattering.
